# LEVEL 1 — SOUND SYSTEM

## Lesson 1.1: The 4 Clicks
**XP:** 10 | **Mode:** Speech Lab required | **Duration:** 8–10 min

### 🎯 Objectives
- Understand that Nama has 4 distinct click sounds
- Identify each click by its symbol: `|`, `!`, `║`, `╪`
- Attempt each click with guided mouth/tongue animation

### 🔤 The Clicks

| Symbol | Name | Articulation (Source Guide) | Kora Tip for Kids |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| `\|` | Dental Click | Tip of tongue behind upper front teeth at gum line. Draw tongue away with lips slightly apart. | *"Touch the back of your top teeth with your tongue tip — then pull away fast. Like a tiny 'tut'!"* |
| `!` | Palatal Click | Forward tongue against roof of mouth, where it curves toward upper front teeth. Sharp downward movement of tongue and lower jaw. | *"Push your whole tongue up to the roof of your mouth — then snap it down hard. Your jaw moves too!"* |
| `║` | Lateral Click | Suction between either side of the tongue and cheek. Lips and teeth slightly apart, lower jaw does not move. | *"Seal the sides of your tongue to your cheeks — then release sideways. Like a horse clop!"* |
| `╪` | Alveolar Click | Tip of tongue between front teeth, pulled back sharply with lips slightly apart. | *"Stick your tongue between your front teeth — now pull it back fast. Pop!"* |

### 🔗 Click Combinations
Clicks can be followed by: a vowel, g, h, n, kh
- Examples from Level 2+ lessons: `!Gâi` (Good), `║goas` (morning), `╪Nû` (sit), `!Gû` (go)

### 🎮 Activities
1. **Listen & Identify** — Kora plays each click audio; child taps the correct symbol
2. **Mouth Animation** — SVG shows tongue position; child watches, then tries
3. **Speech Lab** — Record each click individually; waveform shows spike vs flat
4. **Minimal Pair Drill** — `|` vs `║` (dental vs lateral) — hardest distinction, drill last

### 🔊 Audio Files (Firebase)
- `1-The Dental click.m4a`
- `1-The Alveolar click.m4a`
- `1-The Lateral click.m4a`
- `1-The Palatal click.m4a`

### ✅ Mastery Criteria
Child can produce each click with a visible waveform spike on at least 3/4 attempts per click.

## Lesson 1.2: Vowels & Diphthongs
**XP:** 8 | **Duration:** 6–8 min

### 🎯 Objectives
- Understand Nama's 4 vowel types: short, long, nasalised, diphthongs
- Learn that each letter keeps its own sound (no English blending)
- Recognise vowel length markers (macrons: ā, ē, ō, ū)

### 📖 Key Concept (Source Guide)
> *"In Nama each letter keeps its own sound; for instance in Khoeb the o must be heard separately likewise the e."*

This is critical — Khoekhoē children exposed to English/Afrikaans will instinctively blend vowels. Kora must reinforce the separation.

### 🔤 Vowel Examples from Guide

| English | Nama | Teaching Note |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Son | Axab | a-x-a-b — each vowel separate |
| Come | Hā | long ā (hold it!) |
| Give | Mā | long ā |
| Go away | Bē | long ē |
| Deed | Dīb | long ī |
| Get | Hō | long ō |
| Take | Ū | long ū (like "oo" in "food") |

### 🔊 Audio Files
- `2-diphtongs-!Gae-1.m4a`
- `2-diphtongs-!Gae.m4a`
- `2-diphtongs-Khoeb.m4a`
- `2-diphtongs-Oa.m4a`
- `2-diphtongs-laesen.m4a`

### 🎮 Activities
1. **Long vs Short** — Kora plays a word twice (short vowel / long vowel); child picks which is correct
2. **Clap the Vowels** — Child claps once per vowel they hear (reinforces separation)
3. **Echo Practice** — Kora says, child records, waveform compares vowel length

## Lesson 1.3: The Nama Alphabet
**XP:** 5 | **Duration:** 5 min

### 🎯 Objectives
- Know the 20-letter Nama alphabet
- Understand the 5 non-intuitive letter sounds

### 📖 The Alphabet
`| ! ║ ╪ a B(be) Ts(tse) d(de) e x(ge) h I g(ke) k(kha) kh(khi) m(em) n(en) o p r(er) s(es) t(te) u(oe) w(we)`

### ⚠️ Non-Intuitive Letters (Source Guide)

| Letter | Pronounced as | Memory Trick for Kids |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| C | ts | *"C in Nama says 'ts' — like 'pizza' backwards"* |
| X | g | *"X is a sneaky G"* |
| G | ke | *"G says 'ke' — not like English 'g'"* |
| I | ie | *"Capital I says 'ee'"* |
| U | oe | *"U says 'oo' — like in 'food'"* |

### Examples
- `!Gae` = Fasten | `│aesen` = Sick | `║nae` = Sing | `Khoeb` = Man

### 🎮 Activities

### 💬 Cultural Note
*In Khoekhoē culture, acknowledging others warmly is deeply important. Gangans (thank you) is one of the most used words in daily life.*

## Lesson 3.2: Nouns — Gender & Number
**XP:** 10 | **Duration:** 8 min | ⚠️ Grammar Lesson

### 📖 Core Rule (Source: Proper Nouns section)

Nama nouns carry gender and number through their endings:

| Form | Masculine | Feminine | No Gender |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Singular | **-b** | **-s** | **-i** |
| Dual (two) | **-kha** | **-ra** | **-n** |
| Plural (3+) | **-gu** | **-di** | **-n** |

### 🔤 Examples (Source: Glossary)

| English | Singular | Dual | Plural |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Goat (female) | Piris | Pirira | Piridi |
| Cow | Gomas | Gomara | Gomadi |
| Dog (male) | Arib | Arikha | Arigu |
| People | Khoe-i | Khoen | Khoen |

### 🎮 Activities
1. **Ending Sort** — Kora gives a word; child sorts it into the correct gender box
2. **How Many?** — Kora shows 1, 2, or 3+ animals; child picks the correct noun form
3. **Spot the Gender** — !Gôasab (Sir) vs !Gôasas (Madam) — why do they end differently?

## Lesson 3.3: Pronouns
**XP:** 8 | **Duration:** 6 min

### 🔤 Pronouns (Source: A da !gôa section)

| English | Nama | Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| I | Tita | Always the same |
| You (male) | Sats | Used with/for males |
| You (female) | Sas | Used with/for females |
| We (exclusive) | Sida | This group only — *excluding the person you're talking to* |
| We (inclusive) | Sada | Everyone — *including the person you're talking to* |

### 💡 Cultural Spotlight: Inclusive vs Exclusive "We"
*"In Nama, there are two words for 'we'. Sida means our group — not you. Sada means all of us — including you. Many languages don't make this distinction. Nama does — because in Khoekhoē culture, being clear about who belongs matters."*

### 🎵 The Counting Rhyme (Source: Telrympie — perfect for children!)

```
│Gui - │gam ti ta ge         One, two — it is me
!nona – haka sats ge          Three, four — you are (male)
Koro - !nani, sas ge          Five, six — you are (female)
Hû - ║khaisa ║îb ge          Seven, eight — he is
Khoese – disi ║îs ge         Nine, ten — she is
```

**This rhyme is gold for kids** — it teaches numbers AND pronouns simultaneously, with a catchy structure. Kora should animate this with character illustrations per line.

### 🎮 Activities
1. **Count With Kora** — Animated number bubbles; child taps and says each number
2. **The Counting Rhyme** — Kora sings/chants; child echoes line by line
3. **How Many?** — Kora shows a group of animals; child says the number in Nama
4. **Missing Number** — Sequence with one gap; child fills in

| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 11 | Disi │gui │a | ten + one + connector |
| 12 | Disi │gam │a | ten + two + connector |
| 20 | │Gamdisi | two-ten |
| 22 | │Gamdisi │gam │a | two-ten + two |
| 30 | !Nonadisi | three-ten |
| 40 | Hagadisi | four-ten |
| 100 | Kaidisi | hundred-ten (kai = big/hundred) |

### 💡 Kora Teaching Note

## Lesson 8.4: The Counting Rhyme — Integration Capstone
**XP:** 20 | **Duration:** 10 min | 🏆 Level 8 Finale

The full counting rhyme from the source guide, now understood completely:

```
│Gui - │gam ti ta ge         1 2 — it is me (Tita)
!nona – haka sats ge          3 4 — you are (Sats — male)
Koro - !nani, sas ge          5 6 — you are (Sas — female)
Hû - ║khaisa ║îb ge          7 8 — he is (║îb — masculine pronoun)
Khoese – disi ║îs ge         9 10 — she is (║îs — feminine pronoun)
Disi │gui │a – disi │gam │a sida ge   11 12 — we are (exclusive)
Disi !nona │a – disi haka │a sada ge  13 14 — we are (inclusive)
```

**Hoa da ra!gôa ║khāpesen…!** — *Let us all practise together!*

This is the perfect capstone — it combines numbers, pronouns, gender, inclusive/exclusive we, and pure joy of a rhyme. Children who can recite this have genuinely absorbed the core of the language.
